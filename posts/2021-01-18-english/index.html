Review 2021/01/18 lesson

Vocabulary

  • alternate: verb
    • if two things alternate, or if you alternate them, they happen on after the other in a repeated pattern.
  • reluctant
    • slow and unwilling

Idioms

pull together

  1. if a group of people pull together, they all work hard to achieve something
    • To finish the tough work on time, we all have to pull together.

put our/your/their heads together

  • to discuss a difficult problem together
    • When three people put their heads together, they can solve any problem.

take turns doing something / in turn

  • If two or more people take turns doing work, using something etc, they do if one after the other, for example in order to share the work or play fairly
    • When I was a child, I used to take turns playing video games with my friends in my friend’s house.

play ball (with) / work with

  • to do what someone wants you to do
    • Since he preferred to help another team, he refused to play ball.

do a favor

  • to do something to help someone
    • Since he did a favor for me before, I will help him if he was in a trouble.

real sport

  • someone who cooperates or does a favor willingly
    • The guide was a real sport for bringing me my bag I left behind on the bus even though he had a day off.

bend over backwards (to do something) / go out of one’s way to

  • to try very hard to be helpful, to make extra effort to help or satisfy someone
    • He bends over backwards to satisfy his girlfriend, but it always turns out to be failed because he doesn’t know her wants.

It takes two to tango

  • cooperation is necessary to accomplish something
    • To ease this pandemic, the government and scientists need to pull together, it takes two to tango.

News: https://breakingnewsenglish.com/2012/201231-the-year-2020-m.pdf

For billions of people around the world, January the 1st, 2020 seemed like a great day. It was the start of a year that sounded like science fiction - 2020. Many people had great hopes for the year ahead. Few knew what a rollercoaster ride 2020 would be and how the world would change. The year has been dominated by the COVID-19 pandemic. This has brought hundreds of thousands of deaths, economic chaos, lockdowns and masked populations. Another huge event was the death of George Floyd at the hands of U.S. police in July. This sparked the Black Lives Matter movement and global protests. The year ended with Joe Biden being voted in as the President-elect of the USA.

Different people will have different memories of 2020. Our reporters asked people around the world to tell us what they will remember 2020 for. Ahmed Hussein from Lebanon said he would never forget the massive explosion at a Beirut port in August that killed at least 190 people. He said he thanked God none of his family or friends were hurt, but was sad at the destruction of his city. Lucy Baxter, a nurse in the UK, said her happiest moment came with the news of the vaccines for COVID-19. She said she could see light at the end of a long and dark tunnel. Ayumi Miyamoto of Japan was saddened by the postponement of the Tokyo Olympics but is happy they will go ahead in 2021. She said 2021 would be a good year.
