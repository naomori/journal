2020-12-14: English

December 14, 2020

Review 2020/12/14 lesson: Phrasal verbs Unit 14: Out

Vocabulary

spell out

  1. to explain something clearly and in detail
  2. to show how a word is spelled by writing or saying the letters separately in order
  3. to write a word in its complete from instead of using an abbreviation
  • I got her name on the phone, then I spelled it out.
  • I couldn’t spell his name out, because the pronunciation of his name was difficult.

bring sth out

  1. to make something easier to see, taste, notice etc
  2. to produce something that will be sold to the public
  • I bought an album that my favorite rock band brought out lately online.
  • I was looking forward to a new headphone of apple, however it was too expensive.

sort out

arranged things that were untidy

  • I feel good to sort out my room and I can concentrate on my work.
  • I need to sort out my thinking to calm me down.

clear out

make tidy by removing unwanted things

  • Many Japanese usually clear out their home at the end of year.
  • I need to clear out my desk at the office because I will change my job.

worn out (adjective)

used so much that they had become damaged, from the verb wear out

  • I ran around everyday with worn out shoes, when I was a child.
  • As I worn out my running shoes, I bought a new one through Amazon.

run out of

there was none left because it had all been used

  • As I ran out of medicines for allergy to pollen, I need to go to see a doctor.
  • Since I ran out of medicines for good sleeping, I ought to go to a pharmacy to buy it.

leave out

not include something or someone

  • Since I left out several questions for exam, I will not be able to pass the exam.
  • Please leave me out of the debate, it’s worthelss for me.

cut out

remove by cutting, usually from paper or cloth

  • My mother cut some cloth out of my worn out clothes and made a bag.

show out

lead a visitor who is leaving to the door of a room or building

  • He had urgent business, so I showed him out.
  • As the building is complicated, you must show them out.

see out

go with someone to the door of a room or building when they are leaving

  • Since our customers were going to leave, we saw them out.
  • We always see our customers out due to show our respect to them.

let out

allow a person or animal to leave, usually by opening a locked or closed door

  • Since the cat was eager to go out, I let her out opening the door.
  • Don’t let the baby out because we don’t want him to get injured.

lock out

prevent someone from entering a building by locking the door

  • Although the robber tried to break into my room, I could lock him out somehow.
  • A certain night, a man looked being locked out from the house by his wife.

lock yourself out

accidentally prevent yourself from getting into a building by leaving the keys inside when you shut the door

  • I often hear the story that people have locked themselves out of their room naked after drinking too much when they stayed at a hotel.
  • I’ve never locked myself out at hotels because I’m always careful for that.

Writing: Part2 (11-13)

  1. What is it that your boss lately gave praise to you for?
  • My boss hasn’t given praise to me for anything.
  • I don’t fit in with him.
  • As he is kind of a “know-it-all”, I don’t like the character.
  • Because people who are “know-it-all” don’t study anything and tend to look down at others who don’t know about something.
  1. Which amusement park did you go to recently?
  • I didn’t go to any amusement parks recently.
  • Because I don’t like crowded places. In addition due to this pandemic.
  • I used to go to an amusement park with my family when I was a child.
  • I rode roller coaster and drove a motored cart.
  • I liked it before.
  1. What did you think when you first learned English?
  • I thought English was interesting.
  • Because I felt learning English widen my world.
  • For example, if I can use English well, I can communicate with many people all over the world and enjoy a trip all over the world.
  • Therefore, I wanted to learn English. Still now.

Phrasal verbs: Part 1

015: bring about a change

  • Do you think this new policy will work?
  • Let’s see if it will bring about a change in our society.
  • This pandemic brought about a big change in our society.

016: bring books back

  • When should I return these books?
  • Bring them back by next week, or you’ll be fined.
  • Bring back things I lent you before.

017: photographs bring memories back

  • Tom, take a look at you in these photos.
  • They bring back memories of my childhood.
  • Photographs my parents took brings a lot of memories back to me.

018: bring her back to life

  • How did she survive the drowning accident?
  • Quick action by the paramedics brought her back to life.
  • The call of us brought our grandfather back to life.

019: bring him back to the company

  • I wish we could bring Jim back to the company.
  • There’s nothing we can do now that he quit of his own accord.
  • Nobody hopes that somebody bring him back to the company because he wasn’t good for anyone.

020: bring down the ceiling

  • What happened in the house?
  • The roof collapsed and it brought down the second-floor ceiling.
  • Don’t bring down anything from the top, it’s dangerous for everyone.
