2020-01-06: Touchstone-3 Unit-6 Checkpoint

January 16, 2020

4. What’s going to happen?

  • Cindy: What time does your train leave (leave) today?
  • Dana: I ’m gonna take (take) the 3:30 train. Oh, no, it’s almost 3:00!
  • Cindy: Don’t worry. I ’ll drive (drive) you to the station.
  • Dana: Oh, you don’t have to do that. I will call (call) a taxi.
  • Cindy: No way! I can take you. I ’m gonna go (go) to the mall this afternoon. It’s not far from the train station.
  • Dana: Are you sure it won’t be (not be) a problem?
  • Cindy: No problem at all. I ’m gonna meet (meet) a friend there at 4:00.
  • Dana: Well, OK. Thanks. I ’ll get (get) my suitcase.
  • Cindy: Yeah. We ’re gonna have to (have to) leave right way.
  • Dana: OK. I ’ll be (be) ready in five minutes.

5. A healthy diet?

A. Complete the sentences with different foods.

  1. It’s not healthy to eat too many hamburgers.
  2. If you want to lose weight, eat very few fries.
  3. If you eat too much chips, you’ll gain weight.
  4. You should drink very little beer.
  5. A little nuts every day is good for you.
  6. People should eat less meats and more vegetables.

B. Replace the underlined words in these sentences. How many sentences can you make?

  1. I like boiled eggs better than fried eggs.
  • I like deep-fried onion better than onion saute.
  • I like deep-fried chicken better than stewed chicken.
  1. I drink about three cans of soda a day.
  • I drink about three cans of coffee a day.
  1. I’m trying to eat less ice cream and fewer doughnuts.
  • I’m trying to eat less fat and fewer chips.
  1. I always keep a jar of mayonnaise in my refrigerator.
  • I always keep a bottle of sparkling wine in my refrigerator.
  • I always keep a tube of wasabi in my refrigerator.
  • I always keep a plastic container of pasta sauce in my refrigerator.
  • I always keep a carton of milk in my refrigerator.
  • I always keep a pack of eggs in my refrigerator.
  1. I ate too much candy and not enough fruit yesterday.
  • I ate too much dinner and not enough breakfast yesterday.

6. Get off the phone!

Role play

  • Student A: You are planning a special dinner for an English-speaking visitor to your country. Call your partner to ask for advice about what kind of food to prepare.
  • Student B: Your partner calls to ask for advice just as you are leaving to meet a friend. Try to end the conversation politely.
